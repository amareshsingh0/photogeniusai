# Data Processing Agreement (DPA) – Template (B2B)

**Between:**  
**Controller:** [Customer name and address]  
**Processor:** PhotoGenius AI (“we” / “Processor”)

This template supports compliance with GDPR Article 28 and similar requirements when the Customer (Controller) uses our services that may process personal data, including biometric data in the Identity Vault.

---

## 1. Subject matter and duration

- This DPA applies to processing of **personal data** (including, where applicable, **biometric data**) that we carry out on behalf of the Customer when providing the PhotoGenius AI service (including Identity Vault).
- Duration: for the term of the main service agreement between the parties, and until all personal data is returned or deleted as agreed.

## 2. Nature and purpose of processing

- We process personal data as necessary to provide the service: account and identity management, image generation, Identity Vault (reference photos, face embeddings, LoRA training and inference), consent and audit logs, and support.
- Biometric data is processed only for the Identity Vault feature, as described in our Biometric Data Privacy and Biometric Data Manifest.

## 3. Types of data and data subjects

- **Data subjects**: Customer’s end users (and, where applicable, individuals whose faces are used in Identity Vault).
- **Types of data**: Account data, prompts and generated images, identity metadata, reference photo URLs, face embeddings (biometric), LoRA file references, consent records, access/audit logs.

## 4. Controller and Processor obligations

- **Processor** will process personal data only on **documented instructions** from the Controller (including as set out in the main agreement and this DPA), unless required by law.
- **Processor** will ensure that persons authorised to process personal data are bound by confidentiality.
- **Processor** will implement **appropriate technical and organisational measures** (including encryption at rest and in transit, access control, and audit logging) as described in our security documentation and Biometric Data Manifest.
- **Processor** will assist the Controller in responding to **data subject requests** (access, rectification, erasure, portability, restriction, objection) and in ensuring **compliance** with GDPR Articles 32–36 (security, breach notification, DPIA, prior consultation) insofar as they relate to our processing.
- **Processor** will make available to the Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA and allow for **audits** (or provide third-party assurance reports) as agreed.
- **Processor** will **not engage a sub-processor** for processing covered by this DPA without the Controller’s prior authorisation (general or specific) and will impose on sub-processors the same data protection obligations as in this DPA.

## 5. Return and deletion of data

- At the end of the service or on Controller’s request, Processor will **return or delete** all personal data in its control, in accordance with Controller’s instructions and our retention policy, except where we are required to retain data by law.

## 6. Biometric data (if applicable)

- Where the service includes **Identity Vault** and thus processing of **biometric data** (special category under GDPR Art. 9), such processing is carried out only on the basis of **explicit consent** or another valid legal basis communicated by the Controller.
- Processor will apply the safeguards described in the Biometric Data Manifest (encryption, access control, audit, retention and deletion).

## 7. Governing law and jurisdiction

- As agreed in the main service agreement, or the law of the Processor’s establishment for GDPR purposes.
